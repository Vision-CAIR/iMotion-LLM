iMotion-LLM

Instruction-Conditioned Trajectory Generation

▸ King Abdullah University of Science and Technology (KAUST) ▸ Meta Reality Labs*

Abstract

We introduce iMotion-LLM, a large language model integrated with trajectory prediction modules for instruction-conditioned motion generation. Unlike conventional approaches, iMotion-LLM generates feasible, safety-aligned trajectories from natural-language instructions, enabling context-aware driving behavior. It combines an encoder-decoder trajectory model with a pre-trained LLM fine-tuned using LoRA, projecting scene features into the LLM input space and mapping special tokens to a trajectory decoder for interpretable text-driven control.

To support this framework, we introduce two datasets: InstructWaymo, an extension of the Waymo Open Motion Dataset with direction-based motion instructions, and Open-Vocabulary InstructNuPlan, containing safety-oriented instruction–caption pairs and associated safe trajectory scenarios. Experiments show that instruction conditioning enables trajectory generation aligned with intended behaviors.

iMotion-LLM demonstrates strong contextual understanding, achieving 84% direction feasibility accuracy and 96% safety evaluation accuracy. Our work establishes a foundation for text-guided motion planning in autonomous driving and supports simulated data generation, interpretability, and robust safety alignment testing. Code, datasets, and pretrained models will be released publicly.
